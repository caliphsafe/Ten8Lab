01 · The proposition

This is not another creative workshop.

Participants work through real briefs, shifting deadlines, client feedback, live campaigns, analytics, agency-style roles and public presentations. They learn not only how to make strong creative work, but how to turn that ability into professional value.

“This should not feel like school pretending to be the industry. It should feel like the industry, with education built inside it.”
02 · The experience

Train. Build. Launch. Enter.

Every phase is designed to test craft, strategy, speed, professionalism, adaptability, communication and the ability to recover when the first answer is not good enough.

01 TRAIN

Industry standards, creative fundamentals, audience strategy, business, client communication and professional practice.

02 BUILD

Real briefs, complete campaign systems, team roles, deadlines, critique, revisions and production handoff.

03 LAUNCH

Work reaches real audiences. Participants study live data, defend decisions, test alternatives and pivot when necessary.

04 ENTER

Portfolio reviews, paid micro-commissions, job shadows, interviews, mentorship, referrals and a 90-day opportunity report.

03 · The industry pipeline

Sponsors contribute three currencies.

Money alone does not create an industry funnel. The model asks every serious partner to help fund the work, teach what the industry actually expects and open a tangible door.

$ Money

Stipends, instruction, technology, transportation, media testing and program operations.

◎ Knowledge

Guest teaching, professional critique, mentorship and honest exposure to real industry process.

↗ Opportunity

Paid work, interviews, job shadows, commissions, referrals and concrete career-entry touchpoints.

04 · What success looks like

Measure what happens after the applause.

The program does not stop at attendance or completion. Success is tracked through portfolio readiness, opportunity touchpoints and what participants actually do next.

04
The eight-week journey

From creative instinct to industry readiness.

W1

Talent is not a business yet.

Baseline skills, creative identity, design fundamentals and the three-hour rescue.

W2

Brief. Brand. Audience.

Live client reveal, research, positioning and two opposing creative directions.

W3

Build a campaign, not a post.

Campaign architecture, copy, content systems and the seven-day campaign challenge.

W4

Launch. Measure. Pivot.

The work goes public. Students read live data and adapt when the audience responds.

W5

Welcome to agency life.

Scope, pricing, clients, revision limits, handoff and the messy feedback meeting.

W6

The agency simulation.

Rotating roles, leadership, accountability, role-switch pressure and partner critiques.

W7

The business of being creative.

Portfolio, pricing, discovery calls, proposals, networking and 90-day career plans.

W8

The room.

Professional showcase, live Q&A, portfolio review and opportunity matching.
